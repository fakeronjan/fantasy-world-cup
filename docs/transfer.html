How transfer pricing works

After each round, every surviving asset gets re-priced based on its expected future points (we re-run a 1,000-trial Monte Carlo from the current bracket position). The market shows three numbers per pick:

  • Paid what you spent when you drafted or bought this pick. Locked in.
  • Earned points this asset has scored - yours forever.
  • MV the asset's current "fair" market value: E[remaining points] ÷ 2. The "2" is a calibration anchor (chosen, not measured). It's tuned so that after the 10% buy vig, buyers pay ~1.7 pts/$ on transfers - which matches the game's actual overall average ROI. Example: an asset expected to score 12 more points → MV $6 → Buy $7 → buyer ROI 12÷7 = 1.71 pts/$.
  • Sell what the market pays you to sell (MV − 10% vig, rounded to nearest $).
  • Buy what the market charges to acquire (MV + 10% vig, rounded to nearest $).

Rounding: all prices are whole dollars. We round to nearest (halves go up), with a forced minimum $1 spread either side of MV - so even at low MVs there's always at least a $2 round-trip cost. Example: MV $6 → Buy $7, Sell $5.

Why "Paid" can be much higher than "MV": Initial draft prices cover the whole tournament's expected points. Mid-tournament prices cover only what's left. So Spain's MV after groups will look small - but you've already banked group-stage points AND the asset has de-risked by surviving. Holding is a fair deal. Selling realizes only the residual.

Buy cap: you get 3 voluntary buys total across the whole tournament. Sells are unlimited (the vig already self-punishes). Auto-sells from elimination don't count toward the cap - only buys you actively make. Use them wisely.

Eliminated picks: when a team or player you hold is knocked out, it's automatically sold off your roster and you're refunded 25% of what you paid for it (25% of your purchase price), credited to your balance.
